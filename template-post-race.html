Race Analysis

The Desert Showed Neither Mercy Nor Shade

Two stories ran simultaneously. In the first: Verstappen produced a performance so controlled it was almost offhand — managing the gap, managing the tyres, managing the narrative. In the second: Ferrari imploded with the operatic drama that makes you love and hate them equally. The collision between Sainz and Hamilton on lap 34 will fuel debate for weeks. The desert answered no questions about the championship. It only sharpened them.

The Bahrain circuit at dusk is one of the more beautiful things in Formula 1. The track lights come on before the sun fully sets, and for about four minutes there is a quality of light that belongs to neither day nor night. This palette is that four minutes.

What We're Watching for Next

Round 5 — Miami

Miami is where this season gets interesting. The Hard Rock circuit on the new 2026 regulations is an unknown quantity — the cars are smaller, the active aero changes how they load through the high-speed turns, and the three DRS zones create more genuine overtaking scenarios than any previous iteration of this event. Lando Norris won here in 2024. McLaren understands this track.

Fashion note: Miami week is when paddock style becomes genuinely considered. The heat, the setting, the social visibility — all of it shapes what appears trackside. Watch for the people who dress for the city rather than the cameras. They're usually the most interesting ones.
