The Race

Three Wins in a Row. The Kid Is Serious.

Kimi Antonelli has claimed his third consecutive victory of the season at the Miami Grand Prix, holding off McLaren's Lando Norris in an eventful race that had a little bit of everything — including a 360-degree spin from Verstappen on the opening lap, a safety car, a Charles Leclerc implosion, and a final two laps that the grandstands will remember for a long time.

A dramatic start ensued when polesitter Antonelli faced a three-way scrap alongside Verstappen and Leclerc before both the Mercedes and Red Bull cars locked up into the first corner. Verstappen made contact with Leclerc, sending the Dutchman into a spin that dropped him down the order. Separate crashes for Hadjar and Gasly brought out the safety car in the opening laps.

The lead changed hands numerous times — Antonelli, Leclerc, Norris, Piastri, Verstappen all enjoyed stints at the front. But the pivotal moment came in the pit stop phase. Even if it required a degree of assistance from McLaren's execution errors, Antonelli still had to pounce and pass Norris — and the pass, when it came, was incisive. A subsequent one on Verstappen broke any real tow back to Norris and effectively sealed the race.

"F***'s sake, how did we not win this?"

— Lando Norris, post-race

Norris crossed the line 3.264 seconds back in second. There is a legitimate case that McLaren should have won here for a third consecutive year. A little more precision in execution — team principal Andrea Stella used the words "optimisation" and "adaptation" multiple times in his post-race comments — might have made the difference. The Mercedes was possibly still the faster car, but Antonelli was certainly the faster driver on the day.

The Final Laps

The closing stages delivered everything Miami historically promises. Piastri snatched third from Leclerc during a chaotic final two laps. Leclerc subsequently spun — taking full accountability afterwards — and was overtaken by Russell for fourth. A 20-second post-race penalty for repeatedly cutting corners while dragging his damaged Ferrari to the finish dropped him to eighth. Without an early strategic call that Leclerc himself questioned, perhaps none of it happens. It is, as one analyst noted, a very Ferrari series of events to have occurred.

Verstappen received a five-second penalty for crossing the pit exit line — it didn't change his finishing position. Fifth. From a 360-degree spin on lap one. The best and worst of Verstappen, on full display across ninety minutes.

Fashion Icon

Saint Laurent Won the Miami Grand Prix

Kimi Antonelli won the race. Saint Laurent won the weekend. The French house found itself everywhere in and around the Miami International Autodrome — not because of any activation or buzzy event, but simply because the most stylish people in the city chose to wear it.

Alexandra Leclerc

Fashion Icon of the Weekend

Lewis Hamilton arrived at the track for media day in off-the-runway Saint Laurent menswear from the S/S 26 collection — a neon-orange pullover windbreaker layered over a matching silk shirt and a burgundy tie, with pleated brown trousers and black derbies. Sharp, polished, playful. His best paddock look since joining Ferrari.

But it was Alexandra Leclerc who carried the weekend. On Thursday evening, she hosted an intimate dinner at Casa Tua Miami Beach to celebrate the launch of her collaboration with FRAME — guests included Hailey Bieber, Kim Petras, and Anastasia Karanikolaou. Bieber arrived in a rare Yves Saint Laurent by Tom Ford scarf top from the F/W 2004 archives, sitting next to Leclerc who wore her own FRAME collection debut.

On race day, despite the earlier thunderstorms, Alexandra arrived at the circuit in a full look from Saint Laurent's spring 2026 collection: a sporty windbreaker, a two-tone pencil skirt, and sky-high satin slingbacks in a pale peachy pink. Satin in uncertain weather is a statement. She made it.

Three looks. One house. One weekend. The fashion icon of the Miami Grand Prix 2026 is, without question, Alexandra Leclerc.

The Week Ahead

What This Season Is Starting to Look Like

Antonelli leads the championship with three wins from four races. He has converted his first three pole positions into victories — a record in Formula 1 history. He is twenty years old. The longer this level of performance continues, the less it looks like simply a consequence of having the best package at his disposal.

McLaren have step two of their upgrade package arriving in Montreal next time out, and there is a quiet confidence from within the team about what more is possible from this starting point. Ferrari need to find answers — strategic, operational, and perhaps structural. Leclerc is too good a driver to keep losing races this way.

Next round: Canada. Circuit Gilles Villeneuve. Montreal. The Cultural Grid Preview drops the Wednesday before the race. The Espresso Dispatch this Sunday.
